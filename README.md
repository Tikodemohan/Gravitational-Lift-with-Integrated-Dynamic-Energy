# Gravitational Lift with Integrated Dynamic Energy

## First Year Capstone Project

### Project Overview

Gravitational Lift with Integrated Dynamic Energy is a conceptual smart lift system designed to improve energy efficiency and support sustainable building infrastructure. The project explores the integration of mechanical design, renewable energy sources, and energy harvesting technologies to reduce overall energy consumption in multi-storey buildings.

The concept is based on a dual-lift arrangement where two lifts operate in coordination through an interconnected counterweight mechanism. This approach aims to improve load balancing and optimize the utilization of available mechanical energy within the system.

### Problem Statement

Conventional lift systems consume a significant amount of electrical energy, especially in high-rise buildings with frequent usage. As urban infrastructure continues to expand, there is a growing need for energy-efficient transportation systems within buildings.

This project addresses this challenge by exploring alternative methods of energy utilization through lift balancing mechanisms, solar energy integration, and energy harvesting concepts.

### Project Concept

The proposed system consists of two interconnected lifts. The counterweight mechanism of one lift is designed to assist the operation of the other lift. When one lift moves upward, the other moves downward, creating a balanced movement within the system.

In addition to the lift mechanism, rooftop solar panels are incorporated to generate renewable electrical energy. The generated power can be utilized for operating lift lighting systems, sensors, and other auxiliary components.

The project also includes a power-generating floor/weighbridge mechanism positioned at the lift entrance. This mechanism is intended to explore the possibility of harvesting energy from human movement and foot traffic.
